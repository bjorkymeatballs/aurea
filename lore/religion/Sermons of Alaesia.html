Book I

Persons of the Dialogue: Álláris, Vesrain, Antharis, and Kathyal, other mute auditors.
The scene opens with the group scaling up the stairs to the Temple of Lys during the morning of the eve of Lysandris. They began conversing on their way to the Temple, and they eventually find a rather spacious alcove wherein they sit.

Wherein the House of the Thrice-Blessed Queen of Thoughts Vesrain to me said thus:
"I have gazed streams of immaculate prefection that flow from the Fullness, yet no matter how hard I look, I see him not."

The narrator of the text is referred to as Álláris by all other characters. Curiously, "Álláris" is a title not a name, meaning 'teacher' or 'guide'. The identity of the narrator is not explicitely stated by the text. Based on the fact that the narrator does possess gnosis, it is likely that they are either Elyria, El, or some other emanation of the Divine.
